General Questions

Combined Heat and Power (CHP), also known as cogeneration, is the simultaneous production of electricity and useful heat from a single fuel source. Instead of wasting the heat generated during electricity production, a CHP system captures it and converts it into useful thermal energy, such as steam or hot water, for industrial processes.

In a country facing significant energy challenges like grid deficits and unreliable fuel supply, CHP offers a powerful solution. It provides energy independence from an unstable national grid, reduces operational costs by increasing fuel efficiency to over 90%, and enhances energy security, allowing industries to maintain production even during periods of load shedding or gas curtailment.

Technical Details

Our primary solutions are built on reciprocating gas engines that run on cost-effective natural gas. However, to provide complete energy security, we also offer dual-fuel engines.

A dual-fuel engine is a highly versatile system that can operate on both natural gas and a liquid fuel (like diesel). This allows the plant to seamlessly switch to the backup liquid fuel during periods of natural gas supply cuts, ensuring uninterrupted power generation and complete operational resilience.

Financial & ROI

While the exact ROI depends on factors like facility size, energy usage, and local fuel costs, many industrial CHP projects see a full return on investment within 3 to 5 years. This is driven by significant reductions in energy expenditure (often over 30%) and the elimination of production losses caused by grid outages.
